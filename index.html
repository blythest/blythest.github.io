I'm an information designer and writer, using data visualization as a research tool. I am happiest connecting with people in ways that recognize our unique needs, claims, and contributions to the world we live in. To that end, I'm fascinated by the infrastructure that shape our social existences, whether it's language, maps, algorithms or cloud-based data storage. I’ve spent six years immersed in digital culture, starting with a Master’s in interaction design and data visualization from NYU’s Interactive Telecommunications Program and recently as a software engineer. For four years, I also wrote on arts, fashion, and music for New York Magazine, V, ArtNews, and the Rumpus, among others.
